Redundancy-aware Action Spaces for Robot Learning

Pietro Mazzaglia*

Nicholas Backshall*

Xiao Ma

Stephen James

* equal contribution

Abstract

Joint and end-effector are the two dominant action spaces for robot arms within the robot learning literature. Joint actions provide precise control over the robot's pose, but tend to suffer from inefficient training; end-effector actions boast data-efficient training but sacrifice the ability to perform tasks in confined spaces due to limited control over the full joint configuration. This work analyzes the criteria for designing action spaces for robot manipulation and introduces ER, a novel action space formulation that, by addressing the redundancies present in the manipulator, aims to combine the advantages of both joint and end-effector action spaces, offering fine-grained comprehensive control with overactuated robot arms whilst achieving highly efficient robot learning. We present two implementations of ER, ERA and ERJ, and we show that ERJ demonstrates superior performance, especially when precise control over the robot configuration is required. We validate our results both in simulated and real robotic environments.
